## What Is HWP?

If you have never worked with Korean documents, you may not have encountered HWP. Short for Hangul Word Processor, HWP is a document format created by the Korean software company Hancom. It has been the de facto standard for Korean government, legal, and corporate documents for over three decades.

In South Korea, virtually every government form, official notice, academic paper, and corporate template is distributed in HWP format. The Korean National Tax Service, courts, and public institutions all publish their forms in HWP. It is deeply embedded in Korean digital infrastructure.

## The Problem with HWP

Despite its dominance in Korea, HWP presents significant challenges for modern workflows:

- **Proprietary format.** HWP files can only be opened natively with Hancom Office. Microsoft Word and Google Docs offer limited, often broken compatibility.
- **No cross-platform support.** There is no reliable HWP viewer for Linux, and macOS support is minimal. Most international tools cannot read HWP at all.
- **Not AI-compatible.** No major AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) can process HWP files directly. You cannot paste an HWP document into an AI assistant.
- **No version control.** Like DOCX, HWP is a binary format. Git cannot produce meaningful diffs.
- **Search limitations.** HWP content is not indexable by most search engines or knowledge management tools.

For Korean professionals who need to use their documents outside the Hancom ecosystem, these limitations create a constant bottleneck.

## Why Convert HWP to Markdown?

Converting HWP files to Markdown solves every problem listed above:

- **Universal access.** Markdown files open in any text editor on any operating system.
- **AI-ready.** Feed your converted government documents, legal filings, or reports to ChatGPT, Claude, or any LLM for analysis, translation, or summarization.
- **Version control.** Store converted documents in Git. Track changes across revisions with clean, readable diffs.
- **Knowledge base integration.** Import into Obsidian, Notion, or any Markdown-compatible tool.
- **Web publishing.** Use the content in blogs, documentation sites, or static site generators.
